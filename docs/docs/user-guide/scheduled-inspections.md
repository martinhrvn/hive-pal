# Scheduled Inspections

Plan inspections in advance so they show up on your [calendar](./calendar) and you can pick the best weather window. Schedule a single hive or many at once.

## When to Use It

- You want to plan the week's apiary work around the weather.
- You're coordinating inspections across multiple hives or apiaries.
- You want upcoming inspections to appear on your dashboard and calendar.

## Scheduling Inspections

1. Go to **Schedule Inspection** (`/inspections/schedule`), reachable from the quick-action menu.
2. Select the hives you want to inspect. You can filter and select several across an apiary.
3. Review the **weather forecast** shown for the apiary — temperature, humidity, and wind for the coming days — to choose a good date.
4. Pick a **date** (and optionally a specific time, or leave it as an all-day plan).
5. Add an optional note.
6. Choose how to create them:
   - **Individual** scheduled inspections, one per hive, or
   - A single **[batch](./batch-inspections)** you can run through in one session (give it a name).
7. Submit.

## What Happens Next

- Scheduled inspections appear on your [calendar](./calendar) and dashboard as upcoming tasks.
- When you open and save a scheduled inspection, it is automatically marked **completed** (unlike a manual inspection, which you save as draft or explicitly complete).

## FAQ

**Where does the weather forecast come from?**
From the weather integration tied to the apiary's GPS location. See [Weather](./weather).

**Can I schedule the same date for many hives?**
Yes — select multiple hives, pick the date, and either create individual inspections or one batch.

**Do I have to inspect on the scheduled day?**
No. A scheduled inspection is a plan; you can complete it whenever you actually do the work, or reschedule it.
