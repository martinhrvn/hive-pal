# QR Codes

Print a QR code for each hive and stick it on the lid. Scanning it on your phone jumps straight to that hive — perfect for starting an inspection without searching for the hive in a list.

## What a QR Code Links To

When you generate codes you choose where they point:

- **Hive detail page** — opens the hive's overview.
- **Mobile inspection wizard** — jumps directly into a new inspection for that hive.
- **Audio quick inspection** — opens the [audio recording](./audio-ai) flow for that hive.

## Printing QR Codes in Bulk

Use the print page (`/hives/qr-codes/print`) to lay out codes for many hives at once:

1. **Select the hives** to include.
2. Choose a **size** (small, medium, large) and a **layout** (e.g. 2×3, 2×4, 3×3 per page).
3. Optionally include the **Hive-Pal logo** on each label.
4. Pick the **target** the codes link to (detail page, inspection wizard, or audio quick).
5. Print the page and attach the labels to your hives.

## Single-Hive QR Code

You can also grab a QR code for one hive from its detail page — open the QR code dialog from the hive's side menu.

## FAQ

**Do I need an internet connection to scan?**
Scanning opens a URL to your Hive-Pal instance, so the device needs to reach your server (over your network or the internet, depending on your setup).

**Can I make the codes open straight into an inspection?**
Yes — choose the mobile inspection wizard (or audio quick) as the target when generating the codes.

**What should I print them on?**
Weatherproof labels hold up best on hive lids exposed to sun and rain.
